health planning and management

  • def?

    It is the process of: defining community health problems, identifying unmet needs, surveying the resources to meet them, establishing priority goals that are feasible and projecting administrative action to accomplish the purpose of proposed program.

  • objective (purpose)?

    is to meet the health needs and demands of the people by the following:

    • To match limited resources with many problems.
    • To eliminate wasteful expenditure or duplication of expenditure.
    • To develop best course of action to accomplish an objective.

        • monitoring
          • It is day to day follow up of activities during their implementation to ensure that they are proceeding as planned and are on schedule.
          • It is continues process of observing, recording and reporting on the activities of the organization or project.

          Monitoring , thus consists of:

          1. keeping track of the course of activities
          1. identifying deviations
          1. taking corrective action if excessive deviations occur.
        • evaluation

          Is mostly concerned with the final outcome and with associated factors with it. It measures the degree to which objectives and targets are fulfilled and the quality of results obtained.

  • management
    • def

      It is effective and purposeful use of resources for fulfilling a predetermined objective.

    • basic components (activities)?
      • Planning – determining what is to be done
      • Organizing – setting up the framework and making it possible for groups to do the work
      • Communicating – motivating people
      • Monitoring – checking to make sure the work is progressing satisfactorily
    • what're the types of management methods?
      • methods based on behavioural science
        • Organizational design
          • Health services should be so designed to meet the health needs and demands of people.
          • It should be reviewed every few years because of change in concept ,purpose, problems or technology.
        • Personnel management

          It is skillful use of human resources like selection and training , motivation ,division of responsibilities, incentives and promotions

        • Communication
          • Communication barriers cause delay in reporting, notification and release of supplies.
          • It should be solved by establishing suitable vertical and horizontal communication channels.
        • Information system

          It helps in collection , classification, transmission, storage and display of information. A good IS provides data for monitoring and evaluation of health programs and gives the required feedback to the health administrators and planners at all levels.

        • Management by objectives

          Objectives are set up for different units and subunits , each prepares its own plan of action.

      • quantitative methods
        • Cost effective analysis

          Benefits are expressed in results achieved e.g. Number of deaths prevented, no. of children vaccinated.

        • Cost accounting

          Gives basic data on cost of any program. It helps in cost control, planning and allocation of people and financial resources

        • Input output analysis

          It shows how much input (health service activities which consume resources) are needed to produce a unit amount of each output (such as cases treated, lives saved).

        • System analysis
          • Is essentially finding the cost-effectiveness of the available alternatives.
          • It helps the decision maker to choose an appropriate course of action by investigating his problem,
          • searching out objectives
          • finding alternative solutions,
          • evaluating cost effectiveness of the alternatives
        • Network Analysis

          It is a graphic plan of all activities and events to be completed in order to reach an end objective.

          • what're the two common types of Network Analysis?
            1. PERT ( Program evaluation and review technique) Is a management technique which makes possible more detailed planning and more comprehensive supervision.
            1. CPM (Critical Path Method) The longest path of the network is called the critical path. If any activity along the path is delayed the entire project will be delayed
