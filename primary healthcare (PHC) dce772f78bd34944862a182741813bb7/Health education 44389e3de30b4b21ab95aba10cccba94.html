Health education

  • what's health education ( HE)?
    • is any combination of learning experiences designed to help Individuals and communities improve their health, by increasing their knowledge or influencing their attitudes.
    • It is a systematic, comprehensive and consistent activity Adapted to age, gender, education and particular health, mental or social problems of an individual OR community
  • what's the concept of HE?

    Teaching process providing basic knowledge and practice of health so as to be interpreted into proper health behavior.

    Health Eduction leads to a knowledge-attitude-behavior change

  • how can it help people?
    1. To think about health,
    1. Make decisions,
    1. Acquiring the necessary skills to change their behaviors and to undertake voluntary changes in their health-related behavior.

    So it provides information and advice about health directly to members of the public through individuals and organizations who provide health education to the public.

  • what are the aims of HE?
    1. Health promotion and disease prevention.
    1. Utilization of available health services.
    1. Early diagnosis and management.
  • what are the main components of HE?

      • methods
        • def method?

          refers to ways through which messages are conveyed to achieve a desired behavioral changes in a target audience.

        • what're some common formal teaching methods?
          1. Conference
          1. Workshop 
          1. Seminar
        • what're some common informal teaching methods?
          • health talk
          • brainstorming

            The participants encouraged to make a list in a short period of time all the ideas that come to their mind regarding the problems Without the participants discussing among themselves their ideas and solution on health issues

          • group discussion

            It is collective thinking and exchange ideas shared equally by all the members of the group. Extremely useful in health to solve problems.

          • demonstrations

            basically focuses on practice/skill it involves “Showing how is better than telling e.g. way of preparation of Oral Rehydration Salts .

          • role play

            A type of drama in a simplified manner. It unrehearsed acting out of real-life situations .It is a very direct way of learning; you are given a role or character and have to think and speak immediately without detailed planning

        • what're some points to consider before choosing a method?

          1. How ready and able are people to change?

          2. Your ‘learning’ objectives.

          3. How many people are involved?

          4. Is the method appropriate to the local culture?

          5. What resources are available?

          6. What mixture of methods is needed?

          7. Subject matter

          8. Limitations of time.

          9. What methods fit the characteristics of the target group?

          it is important thing to remember that effective health education is seldom achieved through the use of one method .The best method of teaching combines the various methods.

        • def HLMs?

          are those teaching aids which specifically directed to clearly defined group of audience to give information and instruction about health

        • what're the types of HLMs?

          1. Printed HLMs (such as leaflets, booklets and posters)

          2. Visual HLMs

          3. Audio HLMs

          4. Audio-visual HLMs

          As a media of health education, posters have much less effect in changing behaviours since it is one-way , with no way for groups to participate actively in learning, hence their failure to influence people's behaviour
